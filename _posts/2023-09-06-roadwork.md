Just did some road work

<!-- more -->

Running for a few kilometers at night and ending it with 7x8 parallel bar dips at a park, it feels good to run with no one around. I am also getting better, can now run 4x the distance I used to run when I began running about a month ago (July 31).

<img src="/photos/2023/roadwork.jpeg">

Both running at night and early in the morning does wonders to your mind. Especially for mornings, an early movement primes your mind to be productive for the rest of the day, as well as you get a sense of accomplishment, conquering a task that few people can discipline themselves to do makes you feel like a king. I like to do burpees in the morning or pullups to grease the day with some early victories.

One of the things we suffer from modernity is a lack of movement.

Without movement, there is a lack of the practice of conscious thought, you are running on auto-pilot in a world that's ever increasingly being filled with more distractions, making you more vulnerable to be possessed by the evil spirits of modernity.

Running will give you an arousal for life. I am not even kidding, 2 hours of running a week also improves [your erection](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25801073/). Strengthening one's heart improves one's zest for life, you are ready for opportunities, have stamina for the next battle. You are ON.

Be active. Do more movement. It doesnt have to be running, it can be something else like doing jiu jitsu or some daily physical task like gardening. You want to be awake. Even the great minds of our time were active. Much of scientific work in the past times of Einstein and co., involved collaboration. Reading about how the scientists worked at the [Manhattan project](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manhattan_Project), they would walk around for hours, collaborating with one another, having lunch together to brainstorm. Even hard disciplines like engineering today is 70% collaboration and documentation.

Introducing more movement into your life makes you more energetic and awake, you are able to focus more, [come out with better ideas](https://executiveeducation.wharton.upenn.edu/thought-leadership/wharton-at-work/2018/09/decision-making-and-problem-solving-by-walking/), you are less stressed by the toils of the day, your mind is more ready to take deals and trades, and you are just more configured to take action.

When I began, I couldnt run a mile. I would be out of breath and after the workout, I'd be having pains on my calves or ankles or knees at some point. Then fast forward a month later, and I'm running a mile in 7 minutes, pain-free. I just kept running every other day, that now I can run everyday.

It builds mental fortitude. I have recently began studying algorithms, and I couldnt solve a problem at platforms like [LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/problemset/all/). What I realised was that my [convergence issue](https://ai.stackexchange.com/questions/16348/what-is-convergence-in-machine-learning) was being unable to stay on problems longer. I would often feel disheartened, give up, and just look at the solutions.

What worked for me, was putting the problem in the back of my mind before I set out for a run. Then throughout the run, I would piece by piece lay siege on the problem. Eventually I have developed a mental framework that allows me to better attack algorithmic problems.

There is just something about running that makes it so different to other excursions I've tried like martial arts and lifting weights. It's much easier to see yourself being challenged each time you push yourself further. It's just one simple question every time: how long can I run this for?

It's like with how David Goggins puts it:

> When the ending is unknown, and the distance is unknown
>
> That's when you know who the fuck you are
>
> ([source](https://youtube.com/shorts/5fkhdbG6-wo?si=xhgA_crdTmPIRj7K))

Let's do some road work.
