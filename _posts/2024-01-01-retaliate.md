The last day of the year 2023, I spent it grinding and saying goodbyes. My cousins paid a surprise visit to me after Christmas. They stayed over for a few days. Thus I didnt do much studying and learning as I was cooking, washing dishes, driving them around, all these sorts of chores and of course having fun with them, familial laughter, entertaining, making sure they have a good vacation.

My original plans to grind hard after Christmas met the reality of the spontaneity of life.

Then about a couple days ago I got a new housekeeper for the condo, then 2 days later, she's saying that she's quitting. WHAT? No, I wasn't the problem, I was nice and even cooked her great meals while I'm here. She just said it felt like the job would be boring, and cause she's busted her phone too so she doesnt have anything to do. Wow. I guess what the memes are about people from my generation (Gen-Z) being lazy ADHD-dripped snowflakes is true after all. The hell man. And she's been crying, so I had to be empathetic. I had to arrange for her to go back. And writing this now, she's still with me by the way, she'll probably take a bus in the next couple of days.

Her sudden departure, wrapped in emotions and unforeseen decisions, was yet another facet of the year's unpredictable nature.

What a whirlwind of chores and events by the end of the year.

So I wanted to make the last hours of the year be good. Thus I began grinding.

I had one good last run at the road, did a 6K run. Nobody there with me as I ran through the dark wide park, embraced by the cold evening. From any direction I was easily >1K away from civilization -- it's a big park. Then I got home and started warming myself with the familiar touch of code, just doing some easy programming exercises at a web development learning platform called [freecodecamp](https://www.freecodecamp.org/). And finally, I made for a feast for New Year's Eve: a chicken burger, roasted chicken, and a decadently rich chocolate cake.

Finally about 30 minutes before the clock hit the new year, I had a friend who came by. Uninvited. Surprised me. Heard I was lonely this year and came to say hello. We counted down to the new year and we drank 2 bottles of wine, Chardonnay and Moscato. We had a great time drinking wine and feasting on my modest meal. We joked around and talked of experiences, from flying planes to car troubles. Had some video calls to people special to me. The last few days of the year is the time you remind yourself about who your deepest friends are.

And as the final hours of 2023 dissapear and blend into the next, I stood there at the threshold, thinking about yet another blank page ready to be filled with new stories of resilience, learning, and most especially, growth.

What will 2024 be?

# Retaliation

At the morning hours of the last day of 2023, I came across this great passage from the book Hagakure authored by a samurai named [Yamamoto Tsunetomo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yamamoto_Tsunetomo):

> “Even if it seems certain that you will lose, retaliate. Neither wisdom nor technique has a place in this. A real man does not think of victory or defeat. He plunges recklessly towards an irrational death. By doing this, you will AWAKEN FROM YOUR DREAMS.”

And that is where I decided what 2024 will be molded to.

In the coming year, the quote will be my guiding light, my mantra. The challenges of 2023 - the unexpected interruptions, the setbacks, and even the trivial inconveniences - were but precursors to a greater awakening. They were reminders that life's true essence is not found in unchallenged victories but in how we respond when the odds seem insurmountable.

Take, for instance, my ambitious goal of learning an extensive array of subjects in this single year. To many, this might sound like an insurmountable task, an irrational pursuit perhaps. But isn't it in these seemingly irrational quests that we often find our greatest growth?

2024 will be my year of doing just that - challenging the so-called impossible. It's about pushing past those negative thoughts, the societal labels, and the self-imposed limitations. It's about pursuing my goals with a zeal that borders on the irrational, because often, it's in the irrational that we find the seeds of extraordinary achievement.

Like a samurai facing an unbeatable foe, we must retaliate against every negative thought, every doubt that whispers of defeat, with renewed vigor and determination.

I've spent the past year aiming to be realistic with my goals. But the time for reserveness is over.

Commit to an unwavering pursuit of our goals. Be it mastering new programming languages and human languages, delving deeper into the realms of domains and paradigms of computing, learning a new language, new skill, or pushing my physical and mental boundaries - let us approach each with a relentless spirit. It's not about disregarding wisdom or technique but about complementing them with an unyielding passion and a readiness to take bold, unconventional steps.

Let's make 2024 a year not just of dreams, but of awakening.

Like the surprise pleasant visit of my friend this new year's eve and other events at the end of my year, let us retaliate against the notion of a predictable, safe path. The visit was a reminder that joy can be found in the unexpected, and strength can be drawn from the unplanned. It reinforced my resolve to embrace opportunities that appear out of the blue, and to find fulfillment in the stochastic nature of life.

No modest new year's resolutions and firm goals this year. For instead we shall do everything we can think of. I already have a list of many goals for this year. And every day til the end of the year I'll keep adding more to it, click to open:
