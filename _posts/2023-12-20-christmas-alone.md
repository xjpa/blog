Around half a decade ago I had a blog post about how <a href="/articles/2017/12/25/christmas-family">I spent a nice christmas with my family</a>.

That was 6 years ago. And now I'm alone.

<!-- more -->

I could've been there with them in the Philippines at Mindanao, but I am out here at a condo grinding. Learning about software engineering and AI. Trying to get good enough to build the things I want.

There's only my dog with me. I wanted to be alone this Christmas.

I didnt buy flight tickets. No girl nor friends with me this time.

Cause I want to see what it's like to spend the holiday by myself. I thought it'd feel bad, spending the month alone, but it honestly feels great. And thinking about it, the reason is because I am excited for the future. There's just so many things in store in 2024 for me, that I am drowning in the thoughts of it. My mind's so filled with the excitement of learning all these new ideas and opportunities, that there's no space left in there for negative thoughts.

I am not depressed or anything. Nor am I burning myself out. We're just getting started.

We're locked in. The future is looking great. Out of the dark clouds of the pandemic and its recession, a new era is dawning. Let's go see where this one goes.

I just ordered some spicy chicken sandwiches at Popeyes. Gonna go back to writing some code soon. I'll start working out again. Well I have been working out, but I only do calisthenics. For almost 1 year I've been pumping out at least 80-120 pullups and 300 pushups (in different variants) 5x a week. Hell sometimes I'd do 450 pushups, 10x30 in morning, 5x30 at night. But turns out, it doesnt really help me get big and strong. I fell for the calisthenics meme hard. Running 18 miles/30 kilometers a week and doing 100-120 dips after each run, etc. Sure, I could do one arm pushups. But unless I take my shirt off, you'd think I'm skinny. I had a hard time lifting big things at the spartan race too. I wish someone had told me add some weights. Thus that's why I dont consider calisthenics as a workout nowadays. They're more like warmups. So next year, let's go LIFT.

For now, I'm gonna open this game called [Runescape](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RuneScape). The first game I ever played when I was kid. Let's spend the Christmas there. I always loved the world of runescape when I was a kid, as it's a casual game you can put on the background, then check it a few minutes later to see if your character is done fishing some tuna or chopping some wood. It's idle game and RPG done right. As a kid, it felt so fun to have your character chop some wood for like 5-10 minutes, then just chat with the other players there. It's a semi-idle RPG game done right. I just logged in there the other day and had to create a new account [cause of something from 2010](https://www.reddit.com/r/2007scape/comments/af7b7s/recovering_a_pre2010_account/). Alright, created a new account. Gonna grind it.

I'm gonna do that. Gonna leave my character to fish at Karamja, mine some ores at Lumbridge, or chop some trees to sell. Do those while I go write some code, checking back on the game to go click on a new tree to chop every couple of minutes or more. It's a fun idle game for those who love the long grind, teaching me patience and peace. Maybe next year I'll grind to pick up the guitar again, try to learn to [play a masterpiece](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fAgnRd7BUPo) or two.

Anyway, I wrote this lame anything-goes blog post so that I can have sort of like a weather vane or an ocean buoy for the future. Something to look back to and compare where I stand. Where was I in the Christmas of 2023? What were my young naieveties and cringy thoughts at the time? I could see myself thinking that, the same way I was thinking of where I was a few years ago, skiing with mum and dad and my siblings, having a nice hot pizza during a freezing Christmas.

So to my future self 5 years from now...

Did we win?

I'll do you good. I'm gonna push a lot of code this winter break. I'm gonna keep learning and building that network. I'll get some badass Tom Hardy haircut soon, get back to doing Muay Thai and start lifting. Let's see you do the rest.
