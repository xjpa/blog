Let's stop overthinking for a moment.

Observe the most complex of all beasts: nature.

The grass grows, the trees sway, and the sun graces the sky without pause or deliberation. Nature's rhythms flow unimpeded, akin to a timeless melody sung by the universe. There is no second-guessing in the realm of blossoming flowers, nor hesitation in the rising of the dawn. It is the harmonious symphony of existence, an enchanting orchestra of motion and momentum.

Yet, we, with our complex minds, often find ourselves entangled in a labyrinth of thoughts. We weigh the consequences, ponder the possibilities, and lose ourselves in a maze of contemplation. Our spirits falter, and our bodies grow weary under the weight of overthinking.

But I say to you, my friend, fear not the delicate art of spontaneity. Embrace the vivacious cadence of life's dance, and allow yourself to be carried by its enchanting current. Release the grip of constant analysis and self-doubt, for it is in the freedom of action that we re-discover our inner fire.

Let's go back to our roots. To our ancestors who were glorious hunters and architects of wonders.

# What Should I Build?

Because of my background, I am inclined to say programming, or get some dumbbells and build some muscle, or what I am doing right now: writing. Go start a blog of your own detailing your hero's journey, an epic monomyth that your descendants will read about in the future.

Anything you want. Just build something. A song perhaps or a novel. It's the dopamine hit you get from manifesting something into reality that you are after. No matter how small it is. It is your identity that you are carving as you create.

Just writing 1-2 lines of code today? Fine. I did that in many of my days. My github commits are shit and full of them. Who cares?

Like a wizard conjuring spells from ethereal runes, weave intricate lines of logic and syntax to bring digital wonders to life. Start with simple things, todo app or perhaps a solution to an easy algorithmic problem. Then you can later put your mind into creating an app that simplifies daily tasks, a website that inspires with its beauty, or a video game that takes the mind on a thrilling adventure.

<img src="/photos/2023/build-unity.jpeg">

Or return to the primal art of sustenance: cooking. Bake a cake, or a treat for your dog. Call the boys for a heavy workout like a marathon or a friendly sparring session at the gym, then cook some eggs and a nice steak for them; just like a warrior-king does in the olden times to feed the champions of his tribe. Or cook it for yourself. Don't know how to cook? Neither am I, and that is why I keep trying.

<img src="/photos/2023/depressed-build-something-steak.jpeg">

So heed the call to build something, friend. Whether it's for yourself, your loyal companions, or a treat for your loyal canine friend who'll follow you into the ends of the Earth, let your creative endeavours be a testament to your spirit's resilience and your heart's yearning for fulfillment. For in the act of building, you manifest a piece of your essence into the world, leaving a lasting legacy that echoes through the tapestry of existence.

# /hacking

I am starting the page [blog.johnamata.com/hacking](/hacking) where I'll be blogging about every little thing I'm working on in my journey to learn programming.

Built something simple? Blog about it to get that dopamine rush, and to retain the lessons learned. Followed some tutorial? Doesnt matter, blog about it, I'm sure you learned something useful from that which you'd want to remember for the future. Are you unsure of what you're writing about? Just blog about it, [you do not need to be an expert to start](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=14900376). And if you do make mistakes, make sure to learn from it and correct it. In the end, you are mostly writing for yourself. I doubt anyone reads this blog besides my family and some bots anyway.

Currently I'm visiting the foundations of programming, going over tutorials and books to cover things I may have missed on. A humble start. Let us begin the obsession. And maybe someday, I'll have my answer for what I did about having [a project of one's own](https://archive.is/SNoxQ).

Update: I saw this video quoted at some reddit post and it brings up another cool benefit to writing, and that is that [you learn to structure your thoughts better](https://twitter.com/david_perell/status/1709569520749134043). So go ahead, and start writing. Especially for me, I definitely need a LOT of practice.
