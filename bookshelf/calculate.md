## How to Calculate Quickly (1945)

<img src="/photos/books/how-to-calculate-quickly.jpeg" height="300">

An excellent book full of thousands of exercises (9000+ it says) to get better at speed mental mathematics from addition to squaring numbers in your head faster than somebody with a calculator.

I came across this book when I was younger, around high school. I found a copy of it on the internet and printed it out to solve over a lazy November-December. Speed mental arithmetics is a useless skill, but at the time I thought it'd be cool.

I attribute this book for improving my persistence and working memory. For in the ritual of spending a few minutes a day on this book, I have found the impact of being consistent. And in the practice of solving problems mentally, I have found that it has improved my ability to hold abstractions longer.
