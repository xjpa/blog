## Surely You're Joking Mr Feynman

<img src="https://images-na.ssl-images-amazon.com/images/S/compressed.photo.goodreads.com/books/1504936953i/35167685.jpg" height ="300">

I've always been a fan of reading history and memoirs much more than fictional stories, I only saw the value of reading fiction later in 2023. This book is one of those important memoirs I read as a kid around the early 2010s. I think I first read it in 2011.

Back then, "noone" knew Feynman, there were sparse posts about him on the internet. The only place I could find appreciation of him, was a little forum called [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/) where I found multiple high quality comments quoting and discussing the book.

A decade later, Richard Feynman became more of a pop culture icon, especially for the science nerds. Plenty of science/engineering girls I knew in college started reading his book.
