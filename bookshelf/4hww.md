## Four Hour Workweek (2007)

<img src="https://images-na.ssl-images-amazon.com/images/S/compressed.photo.goodreads.com/books/1442957271i/368593.jpg" height="300">

When I was months away from finishing my CS degree, I used some gains I had from the cryptocurrency market to book myself a flight to Beijing. Costed me around PHP 30K for a round trip ticket, and I couldve saved more if I booked my tickets in advance. I stayed there for 3 weeks and went skiing, ate cheap food like canned food and pizza + mcdonalds. Skiing for a month, the plane tickets, rent, and food, these would cost you under PHP 100k. That's a damn good deal.

A trip to Madrid costed PHP 150k booking advance with a travel agency.

Even if you're an average worker in the Philippines, if you have your finances right, you can save enough to have such adventures in life every year, or save for a "mini-retirement" every 2-5 years.

Travelling made me realise how cheap great adventures are in life.

This book is about designing one's life to optimize for such adventures and opportunities.

So ignore the book's scam-like/get rich title. I assure you, it's much more than that.

This book is lifestyle design.

## The New Rich

A mindset shift about what being "rich" means. Being rich in today's world is not about having lots of money, but instead of time and freedom. Tim (author) advises restructuring life to get the most experiences out of it, not just to accumulate wealth.

## Low Information Diet

Tim (author) coined this term based on the idea that excessive information, especially when it's irrelevant or NON-ACTIONABLE, is detrimental to productivity and mental well-being. In today's digital age, we're bombarded with information from news, social media, emails, and more. Most news are non-actionable, or at least the kind where you're not going to take action of anyway.

We are bombarded with lots of news. Some country is under siege. The president sucks and he's hanging out with some cronies of his. The economy is going down the shitter. You've heard them all before. How about turning off the television and the news for a year, suddenly the world is fine again.

Anything truly newsworthy, you would've known about it anyway through some way.

<mark>My personal heuristic</mark>: if people on the street arent fussing about it, it's not worth spending your time. Thus the last news I googled (writing this it's 2023-10-05) was about COVID-19 in the early 2020s.

## Mini-Retirements

Retirement sucks. We want conquest. Challenges.

Instead of the traditional life model where you work for most of your life to retire and enjoy yourself in the last portion, do "mini-retirements." Propose interspersing periods of work with periods of extended leisure throughout life.

Work for a few years, then take a break for a year to a few months to do something you've been wanting to do. Then come back again to your work/business fully renewed.

<mark>Live Now.</mark> The premise is not to defer life's pleasures. If you want to travel the world, learn a new language, or immerse yourself in a different culture, why wait until you're 60 or 70? What you want in your 20s might be different from your 40s or 60s as our priorities change.

Mini-retirements cost less than an extended retirement. Better to take these mini-retirements that can span a few months to a year, having adventures while learning some new skill or language before getting back to the grind with your mind fiully fresh and upgraded.

## Seek Alternative Education

Built a network, great relationships with professionals and experts in their field, talk to them and get knowledge via osmosis. Get real world experience, a real world MBA education by starting a business, perhaps start with a low-risk business like an online shop. Do online courses and focused workshops. Learn by doing so you can get immediate feedback

## Travelling

Travel light. Use multi-purpose items, learn to roll clothes hyper efficiently (like the way soldiers do it), stick to essentials

Live like a local. Save cost by living like a local. Eat, shop, and rent where they would do it.

Travel off-peak. Lower prices, fewer tourists/more relaxed travel

Stay longer. Hopping around from one place to another costs more. Longer rentals have more discounted rates and allows you to explore hidden gems and off-the-beaten-path spots. Also reduces transit costs, which are a big chunk of travel budgets.

Many of the advices on travelling though in the book, such as booking Round the World travel tickets arent that relevant to today's time anymore. Travel nowadays are cheaper and easier to plan.
