## The Way of Kings

<img src="/photos/books/the-way-of-kings.jpg" height="300">

I bought this book just this month (2023-08), I was about to watch the film [Oppenheimer](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oppenheimer_(film)>) back as I wanted to spend my break seeing something that would get me pumped with pursuing the hard things in life. Eventually I decided to just spend the money on this book. I'm sure Oppenheimer would be a good monomyth, a hero's journey tale that would motivate me[0], but nothing ever beats grand fantasy.

And as it turns out, this book was motivating. As well as beautiful. Check it out:

<video controls ="" width="100%" src="https://xjpa-assets-2023.netlify.app/the-way-of-kings.mp4" type='video/mp4'>
</video>

It's pretty thick, in the video above I compared it to my Games of Thrones book. The total word count for this book is 383,389. Harry Potter (7 books) is 1,084,170 words. So Way of Kings alone, is equal to 1/3 of the whole Harry Potter series. It's even bigger than other recent famous book series like the Hunger Games trilogy (360k words) and The Maze Runner main trilogy (320k words).

The opening chapter of this book was already the beginnings to a call to adventure. The book's motivated me so much, that on the day I got this book, I signed up for a Spartan Race just 3 months away. Me and my pals were talking about the race for weeks but none was budging to sign up until I made the first move to a buy ticket for myself and register a team for us, all thanks from being pushed by this book.

<img src="/photos/books/page/the-way-of-kings-book.jpeg">

It's a tome of courage, will, and a self-manifestation of destiny.
