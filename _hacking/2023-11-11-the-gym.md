So what does this journey entail?

- [Python Programming Foundation - Self Paced](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/courses/Python-Foundation) paid course from GeeksFroGeeks

- [Data Structures & Algorithms in Python - Self Paced](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/courses/Data-Structures-With-Python) paid course from GeeksFroGeeks

- [Coderust: Hacking the Coding Interview](https://www.educative.io/courses/coderust-hacking-the-coding-interview) paid course from Educative.io

- [Elements of Programming Interviews in Python](https://www.amazon.com/Elements-Programming-Interviews-Python-Insiders/dp/1537713949) book recommended to me by a friend

I paid for the courses from geeksforgeeks as getting a certificate in them requires me to solve the programming problems and do well in the contests. It's unlike those from udemy where I can just get a certificate after watching the videos. I know that certificates wont help me with anything, but such an act of gamification incentivises me to do it.

For the educative course called CodeRust (text-based, where you solve problems too on their web IDE) and the last book (EPI-Python), I am still not sure if I'll take them.

I'll update this post as I go through.

Note though, that this is at the bottom of my priorities.

Thus it may take some time and these courses are big. Like for example for the GeeksForGeeks courses, even the python foundation course [is designed for an 8 week study](https://media.geeksforgeeks.org/courses/weekly-plan/Python-Foundation.pdf).
